SPSRL

Open-Vocabulary Semantic Segmentation with Spatial Prior and Semantic Relation Learning

Mingzhu Ping, Jinpeng Ye, Sijia Cui, Di Zhou, Pengpeng Xu, Yan Tian

Abstract

Open-vocabulary semantic segmentation (OVSS) conducts pixel-level classification through textual prompt-driven multi-modal semantic alignment. However, current approaches struggle to maintain their strength when significant visual degradations occur. Although depth information can be generated and fused with visual features, fusion method with both effectiveness and efficiency is difficult to design because of the semantic gap between different modalities. In addition, the disparity between base category training and open-vocabulary inference complicates latent unseen category modeling.

Motivated by the principles of orthogonal decomposition and optimal transport assignment, we design SPSRL (Spatial Prior and Semantic Relation Learning) to simultaneously improve the efficiency and robustness of OVSS, where Visual-Spatial Knowledge Fusion (VSKF) segregates modality-specific features into dual-view vectors to capture distinctive information, and Semantics Relation Update (SRU) discovers latent semantic embeddings in high-dimensional multi-modality features. Experimental results on multiple datasets indicate that our approach improves the mean intersection over union (mIoU) by a margin of 2.6–3.0% compared to the prevailing approaches.
